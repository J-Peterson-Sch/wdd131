Making the Dough and Sauce

Pizza Dough Recipe:

This dough recipe is my favorite. The inclusion of the OO flour helps prevent burning in the oven, but the extra gluten from the all-purpose flour gives it extra stretch and chew. It calls for very little yeast so you will need to let it rise at least overnight in the fridge. If you’re a fan of sourdough crust however, you can leave it in the fridge for up to a week to allow it to develop a richer flavor.

Whisk everything together until homologous.

Add the water to the bowl. Start mixing it by hand using the dough hook. Once all of the water has been absorbed by the flour attach the bowl and hook to the mixer.

Kneed the dough for 7 minuets.

Remove the dough hook and transfer the dough to a lightly greased plastic tub with plenty of room for growth.

Optional: Let the dough rise for an hour or two at room temperature.

Place the tub in the fridge and let it rise for at least 12 hours. It can stay in the fridge for up to a week.

Pizza Sauce Recipe:

This dough recipe is my favorite. The inclusion of the OO flour helps prevent burning in the oven, but the extra gluten from the all-purpose flour gives it extra stretch and chew. It calls for very little yeast so you will need to let it rise at least overnight in the fridge. If you’re a fan of sourdough crust however, you can leave it in the fridge for up to a week to allow it to develop a richer flavor.

Ingredient Amounts:

1 (28oz) can Whole San Marzano Tomatoes

2 Tbs. Olive Oil

1/4 C. Grated Onions (or finly minced)

1 tsp. Dried Oregano

2 tsp. Dried Basil

1/2 tsp. Red Pepper Flakes (optional)

3 Cloves Garlic, pressed

2 tsp. Sugar

1 tsp. Kosher salt

Directions

Sift the flours together into a bowl of a stand mixer.

Sprinkle in the salt and yeast.

Whisk everything together until homologous.

Add the water to the bowl. Start mixing it by hand using the dough hook. Once all of the water has been absorbed by the flour attach the bowl and hook to the mixer.

Kneed the dough for 7 minuets.

Remove the dough hook and transfer the dough to a lightly greased plastic tub with plenty of room for growth.

Optional: Let the dough rise for an hour or two at room temperature.

Place the tub in the fridge and let it rise for at least 12 hours. It can stay in the fridge for up to a week.
